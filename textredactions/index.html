Text Redactions, 2024

I vividly remember learning how to write. I would painstakingly attempt to create letterforms as beautiful and precise as my teacher’s handwriting on the chalkboard. Later in life, I learned that my handwriting is not just about creating recognizable letterforms, but also about identity; it carries norms around sex, gender, and sexuality. Handwriting is now mediated by the new actor of artificial intelligence. Technologies such as Optical Character Recognition are driven by machine learning processes, setting new norms of machinic legibility based on the source material of the training models.

Recently I’ve been exploring AI-based text-removal software. This technology, which is based on a corpus of found handwriting samples, is used in the graphic design industry to remove text from images. I’ve been processing personal handwritten samples—mostly notes and cards written to my mother—through this software. The resulting images are the marks that the software doesn’t recognize as valid language.

Enigmatically autobiographic: I remember these drawings, they are familiar and unfamiliar. To create these works, I animated the results from processing my early childhood wriing samples thorugh the text-redaction software. These works explore the space between legibility and illegibility, meaning and non-meaning. They question the role of languge, memory, and technology within the post-Internet era. The rule is, I animated any mark that isn't reconized as lanaguage by the software.
